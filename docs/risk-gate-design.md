# Risk Gate Design

Risk gates are conceptual review checkpoints used to slow down candidate evaluation and make uncertainty visible.

These are public-safe examples, not real trading rules, filters, thresholds, proprietary strategy logic, or execution conditions.

## Information Uncertainty

Checks whether the candidate is based on incomplete, unclear, conflicting, or weak information.

## Late-Entry Risk

Checks whether the candidate may be reacting to information that is already widely known or stale.

This is a review concept only and does not define entries, exits, or timing rules.

## Volatility Risk

Checks whether the candidate appears connected to unstable or rapidly changing conditions that may make review quality weaker.

## Liquidity / Market-Structure Concern

Checks whether market structure might make interpretation unreliable.

This repository does not include real liquidity metrics, thresholds, or execution rules.

## Event-Driven Uncertainty

Checks whether the candidate depends on an upcoming or unresolved event.

## Source Reliability Concern

Checks whether the source is primary, secondary, unverified, or narrative-driven.

## Emotional / Overtrading Risk

Checks whether urgency, fear of missing out, recency bias, or overconfidence may be influencing the review.

## Review Quality Insufficiency

Checks whether the review note itself is too vague, missing context, or not ready for human decision.

## Design Principle

Risk gates should improve review discipline. They should not be presented as secret trading rules or automated decision logic.
