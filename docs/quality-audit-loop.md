# Quality Audit Loop

The quality audit loop supports post-event learning. It reviews the quality of the original candidate review after the candidate is resolved, outdated, or no longer relevant.

This process avoids PnL, real trades, backtest details, order history, or account data.

## Review Decision Quality

Assess whether the original human decision state was supported by the available context and risk gate review.

## Compare Expected vs Observed Behavior

Compare what the review note expected with what was later observed in public information or general market context.

This comparison is about review quality, not trading profit.

## Identify False Positives

Identify candidates that appeared relevant but later proved weak, noisy, or unsupported.

## Identify Late Candidates

Identify candidates that were captured too late to be reviewed responsibly.

## Identify Missing Context

Review whether the original note lacked key information, source provenance, uncertainty disclosure, or review questions.

## Update Review Checklist

Use audit findings to improve candidate templates, risk gate descriptions, and human review questions.

## Preserve Lessons Learned

Write back lessons learned so future review items can be more structured, less reactive, and more transparent about uncertainty.
